## The Importance of Specialized Trauma Care

Not all fractures are simple breaks. Complex trauma often involves multiple bones, joint involvement (intra-articular fractures), or severe soft tissue damage. Delaying appropriate treatment or opting for unspecialized care can lead to serious complications, including:
- Malunion (bones healing in the wrong position)
- Non-union (bones failing to heal altogether)
- Joint stiffness and chronic pain
- Severe infections (especially in open fractures where the bone pierces the skin)

## Our Comprehensive Fracture Treatment Services

We are fully equipped to handle all types of fractures across all age groups, from pediatric greenstick fractures to osteoporotic hip fractures in the elderly.

### 1. Advanced Diagnostics
Speed and accuracy are critical in trauma care. Our emergency department features immediate access to high-resolution X-rays and multi-slice CT scanners, allowing our surgeons to rapidly assess complex fractures, particularly around joints like the pelvis, elbow, and ankle.

### 2. Conservative Casting and Bracing
For stable, undisplaced fractures, surgery is often unnecessary. We utilize modern, lightweight synthetic casts and functional bracing systems that immobilize the bone while maximizing comfort and hygiene. 

### 3. Minimally Invasive Fixation
When surgery is required, we prioritize minimally invasive osteosynthesis (MIPO). Instead of making large incisions that strip the bone of its blood supply, we use specialized instruments to slide titanium plates and screws under the skin. This preserves the surrounding soft tissues and significantly accelerates bone healing.

### 4. Intramedullary Nailing
For fractures of long bones (like the femur or tibia), we frequently use interlocking intramedullary nails. A titanium rod is inserted into the hollow center of the bone, acting as an internal splint. This allows patients to bear weight and start walking much earlier than traditional plating methods.

### 5. Complex Pelvic and Acetabular Trauma
Fractures of the pelvis and hip socket (acetabulum) are among the most challenging injuries in orthopedics, requiring highly specialized surgical skills. Valli Hospital is one of the few centers in the region equipped to reconstruct these complex injuries effectively.

## Specialized Care for the Elderly

A simple fall can cause a devastating hip fracture in older adults with osteoporosis. Early surgery and immediate post-operative mobilization are crucial for preventing life-threatening complications like pneumonia or deep vein thrombosis (DVT) in older patients.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I do immediately if I suspect a fracture?
Immobilize the injured area, apply ice to reduce swelling, do not attempt to realign the bone yourself, and immediately proceed to our 24/7 orthopedic emergency department.

### What is the difference between a simple and compound fracture?
A simple (closed) fracture means the bone is broken but the skin is intact. A compound (open) fracture means the broken bone has pierced through the skin. Open fractures are extreme emergencies due to the high risk of severe infection.

### How long does a broken bone take to heal?
Bone healing timelines vary depending on the bone involved and the patient's age. Generally, a simple fracture takes 6 to 8 weeks to heal sufficiently, but full remodeling of the bone can take several months.

### Will I definitely need surgery for a fracture?
No. Many fractures can be treated conservatively with a cast or splint. Surgery is reserved for unstable fractures, fractures involving a joint, or cases where the bone pieces are significantly displaced.

### Is physical therapy necessary after a fracture?
Yes. Prolonged immobilization can cause muscle atrophy and joint stiffness. Physical therapy is vital to restore strength, flexibility, and full range of motion once the bone has healed sufficiently.
