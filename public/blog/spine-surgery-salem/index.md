## Understanding Spinal Conditions

The human spine is a complex structure of bones, discs, nerves, and muscles. Over time, or due to injury, parts of the spine can degrade or become misaligned. Common conditions we treat include:
- **Herniated Discs (Slipped Discs):** When the soft inner gel of a spinal disc pushes through a tear in the tougher exterior, it can irritate nearby nerves, causing severe pain and numbness.
- **Spinal Stenosis:** A narrowing of the spaces within your spine, which can put pressure on the nerves that travel through the spine.
- **Scoliosis and Kyphosis:** Abnormal curvatures of the spine that may require corrective surgery.
- **Sciatica:** Pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerve, which branches from your lower back through your hips and buttocks and down each leg.

## Advanced Surgical Procedures We Offer

- **Microdiscectomy:** The gold standard for treating herniated discs. A small portion of the bone over the nerve root and/or disc material from under the nerve root is removed to relieve neural impingement.
- **Spinal Fusion (TLIF, PLIF):** Used to stabilize the spine and alleviate pain caused by degenerative disc disease or spinal instability. We use modern instrumentation to fuse two or more vertebrae together.
- **Laminectomy:** Creating space by removing the lamina — the back part of the vertebra that covers your spinal canal. Also known as decompression surgery.
- **Kyphoplasty/Vertebroplasty:** Minimally invasive procedures to treat spinal compression fractures, often caused by osteoporosis.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most common spine surgery?
The most common spine surgeries we perform are microdiscectomy for herniated discs and spinal fusion for instability. Both are performed using minimally invasive techniques whenever possible.

### Is spine surgery safe?
Yes. With modern advancements like microscopic visualization, intraoperative neuromonitoring, and minimally invasive techniques, spine surgery is safer and more effective than ever before.

### How long does it take to recover from spine surgery?
Recovery depends on the type of surgery. For a microdiscectomy, patients can often return to light work within a few weeks. Spinal fusion may require a few months for complete bone healing, but you will be mobile much sooner.

### Will I need physiotherapy after my surgery?
Absolutely. Physiotherapy is a critical component of your recovery. Our team will guide you through exercises that strengthen your core and back muscles to support your newly stabilized spine.

### When should I see a doctor for back pain?
You should consult a specialist if your back pain lasts more than a few weeks, is severe and doesn't improve with rest, radiates down one or both legs, or causes weakness, numbness, or tingling in your legs.
