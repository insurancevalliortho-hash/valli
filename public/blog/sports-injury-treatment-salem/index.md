## Common Sports Injuries We Treat

Sports injuries can occur suddenly (acute) or develop over time (chronic/overuse). Our team is highly experienced in diagnosing and treating a wide array of conditions, including:

- **Ligament Tears (ACL, PCL, MCL, LCL):** The knee is particularly vulnerable during high-impact sports. A torn Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) is one of the most common and devastating injuries for athletes.
- **Meniscus Tears:** The shock-absorbing cartilage in the knee can easily tear during twisting motions.
- **Rotator Cuff Injuries:** Common in sports requiring overhead motions like tennis, cricket, and swimming.
- **Shoulder Dislocations and Instability:** High-impact collisions can force the shoulder joint out of its socket, requiring specialized stabilization.
- **Ankle Sprains and Fractures:** The most frequent injury in court and field sports.
- **Tennis Elbow & Golfer's Elbow:** Overuse injuries causing pain in the tendons around the elbow joint.

## Advanced ACL Tear Surgery in Salem

An ACL tear doesn't mean the end of your athletic career. At our **sports medicine clinic**, we perform state-of-the-art **ACL tear surgery in Salem**. 

Instead of traditional open surgery, we utilize **Arthroscopy**—a minimally invasive surgical technique. By making tiny keyhole incisions, our surgeons insert a miniature camera (arthroscope) and specialized instruments to reconstruct the torn ligament using a graft (usually taken from the patient's own hamstring or patellar tendon). 

**The benefits of arthroscopic ACL reconstruction include:**
- Significantly smaller scars
- Less post-operative pain
- Lower risk of joint stiffness
- Faster rehabilitation and return to sport

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is arthroscopic surgery?
Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure where a surgeon examines and repairs joint damage through a small incision using a camera (arthroscope) and specialized surgical tools.

### How long does it take to recover from an ACL tear?
Recovery timelines vary, but most athletes can return to light training within 4-6 months and full competitive sports within 9-12 months, provided they strictly follow their physiotherapy protocol.

### Do all meniscus tears require surgery?
No. Small, peripheral tears in the "red zone" (where there is good blood supply) may heal with conservative management. However, larger or complex tears often require arthroscopic repair or trimming.

### What is PRP therapy?
Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy involves drawing a small amount of your blood, concentrating the platelets, and injecting them into the injured tendon or joint to stimulate and accelerate the body's natural healing process.

### Can I prevent sports injuries?
While not all injuries are preventable, you can significantly reduce your risk by maintaining proper conditioning, warming up adequately, using the correct equipment, and avoiding sudden spikes in training intensity.
